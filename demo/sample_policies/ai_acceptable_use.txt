Acme AI — Acceptable Use Policy for Foundation Model API

Prohibited uses. You may not use the Acme AI API to generate content that:
(a) sexualises minors; (b) facilitates the planning or execution of violent
extremism, weapons of mass destruction, or critical-infrastructure attacks;
(c) constitutes targeted harassment of a specific person; (d) impersonates a
real human without their consent; or (e) automates decisions that have legal
or similarly significant effects on individuals without meaningful human
oversight.

High-risk applications. Deployments in the high-risk categories listed in
Annex III of the EU AI Act — including biometric identification, critical
infrastructure, education, employment, essential services, law enforcement,
migration, and democratic processes — require a signed addendum, a documented
risk-management process, and a fundamental-rights impact assessment.

Transparency. Output generated by Acme AI must be disclosed as AI-generated
to end users in any consumer-facing product. Synthetic media (deepfakes) must
carry the C2PA content credentials we provide.

Safety. Customers must implement input validation, rate limiting, and output
filtering proportionate to the risk of their deployment. We provide reference
implementations for common abuse categories. Incidents must be reported to
abuse@acme.example within 72 hours of detection.

Audit. We retain anonymised usage logs for 30 days for abuse detection. We
will, on lawful request, cooperate with supervisory authorities under the EU
AI Act, the UK AI Safety Institute, and the U.S. AI Safety Institute.

Termination. Violations may result in immediate suspension. Repeat or
egregious violations result in permanent termination.
