PRIVACY POLICY

Last updated: 2026-01-15

1. WHO WE ARE

Acme Analytics GmbH ("we", "us", "our") is the data controller for the
personal information we process about you. You can contact our Data
Protection Officer at privacy@acme-analytics.example, or by post at
Acme Analytics GmbH, 12 Friedrichstrasse, 10117 Berlin, Germany.

2. INFORMATION WE COLLECT

We collect the following categories of personal information:
  - Account data: name, email address, hashed password.
  - Usage data: IP address, browser type, pages visited, session duration.
  - Billing data: company name, billing address, VAT number, payment token.
  - Support data: any message you send us via support@acme-analytics.example.

3. PURPOSE OF PROCESSING AND LAWFUL BASIS

We process your personal data for the following purposes, each with a
documented lawful basis under Article 6 of the GDPR:

  (a) To provide the service you requested — performance of contract.
  (b) To bill you and collect payment — performance of contract.
  (c) To prevent fraud and abuse — legitimate interest.
  (d) To send service announcements — legitimate interest.
  (e) To send marketing emails — consent (opt-in, withdrawable any time).
  (f) To comply with tax, accounting and audit obligations — legal obligation.

4. RETENTION PERIOD

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purpose for
which it was collected:
  - Account data: for the duration of the account, then 90 days after closure.
  - Billing data: 10 years (statutory tax-records retention period).
  - Usage logs: 13 months from the date of collection.
  - Support tickets: 2 years from the last message in the ticket.

5. YOUR DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

Under Articles 15 to 22 of the GDPR you have the right of access to your
data, the right to rectification, the right to erasure (also known as
the right to be forgotten), the right to data portability, the right to
restrict processing, and the right to object. You also have the right
to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority. You may
exercise any of these rights by emailing dpo@acme-analytics.example.

6. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

When we transfer personal data outside the European Economic Area, we
rely on the Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European
Commission, supplemented by transfer impact assessments. A list of the
third countries to which we transfer data is available on request.

7. DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION

In the unlikely event of a personal data breach that is likely to result
in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the competent
supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach,
in accordance with Article 33 of the GDPR. Affected individuals will
also be notified without undue delay.

8. THIRD-PARTY PROCESSORS

We share personal data with the following processors, each bound by a
written data processing agreement: Stripe (payments), AWS Frankfurt
(hosting), Postmark (transactional email), and Sentry (error logging).

9. COOKIES

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies. See our Cookie Policy
for details and how to opt out.

10. CHANGES TO THIS POLICY

We will notify you of material changes 30 days before they take effect.
